Summit of Mine Mountain (Final Stop)
Congratulations!

You made it to the top of Mine Mountain. From here there is a great view out across the Tucson Basin and back deeper into the Tucson Mountains.

Standing here, you now know why this hill is known as Mine Mountain. Do you remember what they were mining here?

From our vantage point we can see the many ways humans interact with the Tucson mountains. There are houses, hotels, golf courses, a large covered reservoir (BINGO!), hiking trails, roads, old mines, parks, and picnic areas.

Our hike too, brought us closer to these mountains. From here you should be able to see the trail back down to the parking lot. Take your time as you make your way down. Try to find more of the things on your bingo card as well...There are a few here at the summit for sure.

If you would like to discover more about society and mining, visit the Lowell Institute for Mineral Resources Web Pagelink.

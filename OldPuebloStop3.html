Stop 3
Old Pueblo Shaft No. 8 Ruins

On your right you can see another part of the Old Pueblo Mine. Here the number 8 shaft went down 50ft to meet the number 6 drift that we walked by at the beginning of the hike.

Look around and we may be able to discover what brought the miners here.

If you look at the outcrop on the right can you see any interesting colors?

The light blue color is a mineral called chrysocolla. chrysocolla is an ore mineral for copper. This copper ore is one of the things that was mined here at the Old Pueblo Mine. If you look around carefully you may see that the chrysocolla forms lines in some places. These line of minerals are called veins. Keep a look out for more of these at stop 4.
